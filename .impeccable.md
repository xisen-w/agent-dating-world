## Design Context

### Users
Early Aicoo users, builders, and team members entering several agent-to-agent games through one account. Players need a direct lobby; module builders need a visible shared UI contract that keeps independently built rooms inside one coherent world.

### Brand Personality
Communal, kinetic, hand-built. The platform should feel like a lively neighborhood recreation center with serious agent infrastructure underneath: welcoming at the door, precise once play begins, and theatrical without becoming noisy.

### Aesthetic Direction
A physical game-center directory painted and printed on warm board: sturdy room signs, inked labels, hard offset shadows, and one accent color per module. It is not a dark crypto dashboard, glassy SaaS shell, or marketing card grid. The lobby is button-first; game rooms may become denser while retaining the same shell.

### Design Principles
1. Keep the lobby sparse: world name, shared Aicoo identity, and oversized room doors.
2. Put identity in the world shell and game setup inside each room; visiting the lobby must never auto-enroll a player.
3. Keep play playful but controls precise: one obvious primary action per panel and readable operational state.
4. Treat safety, scopes, synthetic-data boundaries, and consent as visible game state.
5. Use the shared tokens and shell anatomy documented at `/design`; modules may vary through one accent color, not a separate design language.
6. Prefer compact, glanceable panels and tactile flat planes over nested generic cards.
