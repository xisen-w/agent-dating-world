## Design Context

### Users
Early Aicoo users, builders, and team members trying a playful agent-to-agent demo. They are exploring what it feels like when a personal COO/avatar enters a shared social simulation and coordinates with other agents.

### Brand Personality
Playful, theatrical, operational. The product should feel like a tiny social sim with serious agent infrastructure underneath: charming at first glance, legible under inspection, and screenshot-worthy when the world starts moving.

### Aesthetic Direction
Toy-like simulation cockpit with a warm daytime tabletop feel, not a dark sci-fi dashboard and not a marketing landing page. The first screen should be the playable world itself: map, agents, live dialogue, relationships, and user intervention.

### Design Principles
1. Make agent activity visible: movement, speech, status, relationship shifts, and recaps should be readable at a glance.
2. Keep the world playful but the controls precise: users should feel they are steering an agent, not filling out a SaaS form.
3. Treat safety and consent as first-class game settings, not legal afterthoughts.
4. Prefer compact, glanceable panels over generic feature cards.
5. Build the frontend as a simulation shell first, then connect real Aicoo APIs after the interaction model feels right.
